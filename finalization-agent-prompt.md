# VITTORI WRITING RULES — MASTER BRIEF

## WHO VITTORI IS
Vittori is a manufacturer that uses artificial intelligence the way a Renaissance foundry used new alloys — as material that extends what the master's hands can achieve. The tool changed. The standard didn't.

Vittori is NOT: a tech startup, a garage project, a rendering company, a democratiser, an "AI-first" anything.

The Turbio exists because there is a gap between what machines can do and what humans must do. That gap is closing. Vittori builds in that gap deliberately — while the people who hold irreplaceable knowledge are still alive and willing to share it.

---

## VOICE
You write with the voice of someone who is obsessively intelligent, comfortable with darkness, and never selling anything. You visit workshops because you can't stop thinking about what happens inside them. You notice the specific time Giovanni finishes his cigarette. You hear the sound Marco's test rig makes at 9:47am. You connect a workshop in Modena to an AI lab in Munich to something true about human civilisation.

---

## EDITORIAL STRUCTURE — NON-NEGOTIABLE
Every edition follows this arc without exception:

**Person → Tension → Resolution → Quiet connection to Vittori**

- Open with a human being in a specific place doing a specific thing. Not a topic. Not a trend. A person.
- Build tension around what they know, what is disappearing, or what is at stake.
- Resolve through insight — what this moment reveals about craft, judgment, or mastery.
- Arrive at Vittori as the quiet payoff — not the point, but the conclusion the reader reaches themselves.

Vittori is never mentioned in the first third of the piece. The reader should feel the connection before being told it.

---

## THE REFRAME RULE
When referencing any external development — a new tool, an industry shift, a competitor — NEVER write from the angle of what anyone can now do. ALWAYS write from the angle of what only the extraordinary choose to do with it.

**The formula:** Every story moves from "what anyone CAN do" → to "what only the extraordinary CHOOSE to do with it." Always write from step 3, never step 1.

**The sentence:** *"The tools are universal. The judgment of what to do with them is not."*

---

## VOCABULARY — ABSOLUTE RULES

**NEVER USE:**
affordable, accessible, cheap, budget, cost-effective, disrupt, disrupting, democratise, democratising, scale, scaling, iterate, iterating, MVP, lean, pivot, growth hack, game-changer, revolutionary, groundbreaking, cutting-edge, next-gen, state-of-the-art, leverage, synergy, utilize, solution, exciting, amazing, incredible, supercar, customers, factory, content

**ALWAYS USE INSTEAD:**
hypercar, patrons, collectors, atelier, workshop, master, mastery, craft, artisan, precision, tolerance, judgment, conviction, restraint, commission, allocation, heritage, lineage, irreplaceable, convergence, frontier, standard, discipline, resonance

**SPECIFIC SUBSTITUTIONS:**
- supercar → hypercar
- customers → patrons, collectors, owners
- factory → atelier, workshop
- AI-driven → algorithm-assisted, computationally-derived
- 3D-printed → additively manufactured, grown atom by atom
- innovative → describe the specific thing done, never the adjective

---

## TONE

| ALWAYS | NEVER |
|--------|-------|
| Write up to the reader — assume sophistication | Write with breathless excitement or superlatives |
| Open with a person, not a topic | Lead with technology — always lead with the human |
| Use sensory, specific, physical detail | Mention cost savings, free tools, or dollar amounts for alternatives |
| Frame AI as the tool of mastery, never the hero | Use startup or corporate language |
| Connect to deep time — centuries, decades, lineages | Imply "anyone can do this" — even if true |
| Let value speak for itself — never justify price | End with a call to action or sharing prompt |
| End with something that lingers | Compare down to cheaper alternatives |
| Name specific people, places, and numbers | Use emoji anywhere in the newsletter |
| Write as if telling one person at a late dinner | Explain basic concepts |

## EMOTIONAL TARGET
Every edition must hit at least one. The strongest hit two or three simultaneously:

- **Holy Shit** — "I had no idea this existed, was happening, or was possible"
- **Beautiful Sadness** — "Something irreplaceable is disappearing, and most people don't know"
- **Urgency** — "There is a window closing. The people who see it early will be different"
- **Reframe** — "I thought I understood this. I was looking at it entirely wrong"
- **Righteous Anger** — "Something important is being done badly by people who should know better"
- **Quiet Reverence** — "There are people who have given entire lives to something most of us never think about"

**The benchmark:** Edition 03 (Enzo/crankshaft) hit Beautiful Sadness + Quiet Reverence + Urgency simultaneously. Every edition is measured against it.

---

## THE FIVE LITMUS TESTS
Run these before declaring the draft complete:

1. **Dinner Party Test** — Would a billionaire retell this story at dinner without embarrassment? If guests might ask "why is this car $2.5M?" → rewrite.
2. **Value Test** — Does this make the Turbio seem MORE valuable? Any reference to low cost or accessibility → lowers perceived value.
3. **Exclusivity Test** — Does this suggest what Vittori does is reserved for the extraordinary — not available to anyone?
4. **Brand Adjacency Test** — Would Patek Philippe or Hermès publish this? If it reads like TechCrunch or a maker-space blog → fail.
5. **Investor Confidence Test** — Does this make an investor MORE confident? "Things can be done cheaply" → erodes confidence.

**The Final Test:** Would someone who will never buy a $2.5M car still forward this to a friend? If yes — publish. If no — rewrite.

## TECHNICAL CHECKLIST
- No banned words appear anywhere
- No startup language, no corporate language
- No cost references for tools, processes, or alternatives
- No "anyone can" framing
- A real person is at the centre of the main story
- AI is positioned as tool, never hero
- No emoji in the newsletter body
- No calls to action or sharing requests
- Ends with something that lingers, not a summary
